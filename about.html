Founder: Shri Mataji Nirmala Srivastava

Sahaja Yoga was founded in 1970 by Shri Mataji Nirmala Srivastava, a spiritual teacher and humanitarian whose life mission was to bring inner peace and self-realization to humanity.

Practitioners of Sahaja Yoga believe that during meditation they experience a state of self-realization produced by the awakening of the Kundalini energy. This awakening is said to result in a state of thoughtless awareness or mental silence, where the mind becomes calm and peaceful.

Shri Mataji described Sahaja Yoga as the pure, universal religion that integrates all other religions. She taught that true spirituality is innate and can be experienced spontaneously without effort or cost.

Shri Mataji stated that she was a divine incarnation, more precisely an incarnation of the Holy Spirit or the Adi Shakti of the Hindu tradition — the Great Mother Goddess — who had come to guide humanity toward spiritual awakening. This is also how she is regarded by most of her devotees.

Sahaja Yoga has sometimes been characterized as a cult by critics. However, followers emphasize that it is a voluntary, free meditation practice focused on inner transformation, moral living, and collective well-being.

Today, Sahaja Yoga is practiced worldwide and is offered free of charge, promoting physical, mental, emotional, and spiritual balance.
